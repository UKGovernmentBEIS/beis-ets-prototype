Manage your carbon emissions using ETS: step by step

Check what you need to do to be compliant for Emissions Trading System.

  1. Step 1 Check if you need you be part of the scheme

    If you’re business activities pollutes it’s likely that you needs to be part of the scheme

    1. Find out what is Emissions Trading System
    2. Check if you need to comply as an installation
    3. Check if you need to comply as an aircraft operator

  2. Step 2 Get your Greenhouse Gas permit or aviation emission plan

    1. Guidance to opening a ETSWAP account
    2. Apply for Greenhouse gas permit
    3. Apply for aviation emission plant
    4. Pay ETS fees and charges for ETSWAP

  3. Step 3 Get your ETS Registry account

    Not all installations and aviation operator will need to manage ETS allowance. Some operators are exempt or follow a simplified procedure.

    1. Check if you qualify for small emitters or Hospital Opt Out scheme
    2. Check if you qualify for simplified procedure
    3. Apply for a Registry account
    4. Pay ETS fees and charges for Registry

  4. and Get your free allowance allocation

    Some installation and aviation will qualify for free allowance allocation.

    1. Free allowance allocation
    2. Get free allowance for new operators from New Entrant Reserve (NER)

  5. Step 4 Monitor your emissions

    You will need to monitor your emission for the current reporting year in accordance with your approved monitoring or emissions plan.

    1. Monitor your emissions

  6. Step 5 Making changes to your permit or monitoring plan.

    If your business activities changes and you increase or decrease capacity or production, you may need to make changes to your permit or monitoring plan. This may also affect your free allowance allocation.

    1. Notify your regulator about the changes
    2. Check eligibility for more allowance from New Entrant Reserve

  7. Step 6 Reporting and Verifier your emissions by 31 March each year

    Most operators will need to appoint a verifier that is accredited for the regulated activity you are reporting on. In some instances, you do not need an accredited verifier

    1. Check if you need a verifier
    2. Choose an accredited verifier
    3. Organise your verifier site visits to your installation or aircraft operator

  8. Step 7 Submit your emissions by 31 March each year

    1. Submit emissions data

  9. Step 8 Buy or sell allowances

      You can buy more allowance if you don’t have enough to surrender or sell excess allowance to someone else. Make sure you have enough allowance to meet your obligation by 30 April each year.

    1. Buy more allowance
    2. How to trade and transfer your allowance
    3. Check and transfer your allowance

  10. Step 9 Surrender your allowances by 30 April each year

    1. Surrender your allowance

  11. Step 10 Complete your improvement reports by 30 June each year

    You need to regularly check whether you can improve the way you monitor your emissions. How frequently you need to do this will depend on your installation category and whether your verifier suggested any improvements.

    1. Submit your improvement report
